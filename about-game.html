ABOUT CONWAY'S GAME OF LIFE
© Copyright 2013                 All Rights Reserved                  Varun Verma                        info@varunverma.net
The game of life is a grid based game. It is played on a grid of 30 by 30 on this website. The grid consists of cells where each cell is either alive or dead. In this implementation the grid is wrapped around so each of the cell in the grid has 8 neighbours.

At each step in time, following given transitions occur:
  1. Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if by isolation.
  2. Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
  3. Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overcrowding.
  4. Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by                     
      reproduction

Each cell has a colour:either red, green or grey. Dead cell has colour grey where are live cells are either red or green. When a new cell is born it inherits majority colours of its surrounding cells. At the initial stage left mouse click can be used to make a specific cell red and righ mouse click make a cell green. Clicking on the middle button or the scroll button would make the cell grey (i.e. dead).
